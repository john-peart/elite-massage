Services

The practice of massage therapy is the assessment of the soft tissue and joints of the body and the treatment and prevention of physical disfunction and pain of the soft tissue and joints by manipulation to develop, maintain, rehabilitate or augment physical function, or releave pain.

Massage Therapy Act, 1991.

We can help with:

  • Soft tissue (muscle, tendon, joint and nerve) injuries
  • Sports injuries
  • Overuse and repetitive strain injuries
  • Generalized muscle tension
  • Pre or Post surgical rehabilitation
  • Arthritis
  • Headaches
  • Edema (swelling)
  • Pregnancy related problems
  • Motor vehicle accident injuries
  • Stress and anxiety

Our Staff

Elite Massage Therapy was opened by Scott Green in June of 2009. The massage and rehabilitation clinic is located at 386 Plains Road East in Burlington's beautiful Aldershot community.

Our small clinic is comprised of two treatment rooms, reception area and is also wheelchair accessible. Elite massage provides a quiet, relaxing and comfortable atmosphere where our team of therapists' focus is on providing top quality treatments for everyone.

All our practitioners are Registered Massage Therapists and members of the College of Massage Therapists of Ontario. This body is mandated and overseen by the Regulated Health Care Professional Act and serves as a governing organization for the profession of massage therapy. As members, we strive to provide you with the latest information to ensure that you receive the treatment that is right for you.

Scott Green - Registered Massage Therapist

During my several years in practice as a Registered Massage Therapist, I have been heartened to relieve hundreds of clients from their health conditions and injuries. It has been gratifying to have a career that makes a difference in people’s lives.

I graduated from the Canadian Therapeutic College with honours in 2001. After graduating, I successfully completed my exams through the College of Massage Therapists of Ontario.

In 2001, I started working at Brant 730 Physiotherapy were I worked for three years honing my massage therapy skills. During this time I was able to work alongside physiotherapists where I gained a vast knowledge in other therapeutic areas.

In 2004, I joined the Burlington Family Care and Rehabilitation Clinic. I was fortunate enough to work out of this clinic for the next five years, gaining much more experience in massage therapy as well as working alongside a very knowledgeable chiropractor.

In June of 2009, my dream came true of opening up my own clinic… “Elite Massage Therapy”! I have now been practicing since 2001; and have a special interest in deep tissue and therapeutic massage as well as injury rehabilitation. The bottom line for me is getting my clients the results they desire.

During my time as a massage therapist I have gained great experience by volunteering for three seasons for the Burlington Braves football club, where I was trained in techniques such as injury assessments, taping and bracing as well as on and off the field injury care. I was also able to work as a teaching assistant at the Canadian Therapeutic College where I was able to take an active approach to helping other students study in the Massage therapy field.

Stephanie Brueschke - Registered Massage Therapist
